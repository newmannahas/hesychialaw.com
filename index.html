The Foundation

As an undergrad, I took courses across thirteen disciplines—from upper-level physics to modern poetry—and graduated first in my class. My post-grad research at Oxford focused on issues at the intersection of science, philosophy, and religion.

The Legal Journey

At Harvard Law School, I served as a teaching fellow for Michael Sandel's "Justice." That experience impacted how I think about the law and persuasion perhaps more than any single course at law school. Since then, I've worked at America's top law firms, spanning multiple jurisdictions, industries, and practice areas.

The Approach in Action

When plaintiffs filed a securities class action against BofA seeking $10B in damages, I devoured accounting literature to develop a novel argument that got the case dismissed before a single document was produced.

When I joined—on the eve of trial—the team enforcing a Honeywell patent, I quickly translated complex electricity and magnetism issues into jury-friendly terms.

The constant has been the creativity and interdisciplinary perspective I bring to solving complex problems.
